Speedrunning Crimson Uplink: Notes From the Echo Loop Community

Players have started pushing Crimson Uplink for time, not just score — here's what they've learned.

It didn't take long after Crimson Uplink landed on Echo Loop for players to start timing their runs instead of just chasing points. That's usually a good sign — it means the underlying mechanics are consistent enough to be optimized, which isn't true of every game that gets added to the library.

The early routes are rough, mostly built on trial and error, but a few patterns have already emerged. Efficient players tend to commit to a decision early and stick with it rather than second-guessing mid-run — hesitation costs more time than a wrong turn ever does.

Echo Loop doesn't run official leaderboards for every title yet, but the community has been keeping its own records in the meantime. If Crimson Uplink is on your list, it might be worth timing your next attempt — you may already be closer to a personal best than you think.
